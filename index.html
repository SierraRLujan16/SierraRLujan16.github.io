Meatloaf

  • Prep Time: 10 minutes
  • Cook Time: 50 minutes
  • Rest Time: 10 minutes
  • Total Time: 1 hour and 10 minutes
  • Serves: 8
Equipment
  • Baking sheet
  • Frying pan
  • Mixing bowls
    		•
    Ingredients
  • 1/2 medium onion
  • 1 teaspoon butter
  • 2 eggs
  • 3/4 cup milk
  • 3/4 cup italian bread crumbs
  • 2 pounds lean ground beef
  • 1 tablespoon ketchup or chili sauce
  • 1 teaspoon italian seasoning
  • 2 teaspoon dried parsley
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon black pepper
    		•
    Sauce
  • 1/2 cup chili sauce
  • 1/2 cup kethchup
  • 2 tablespoons brown sugar
    		•

    Step 1: Preheat oven to 350F Degrees. Line a rimmed baking pan with aluminum foil and spray with cooking spray.

    Step 2: In a small pan, cook onions in butter over medium low heat until tender. Let them cool completely.

    Step 3: In a medium bowl, combine eggs, milk and breadcrumbs. Let the mixture sit for 5-10 minutes.

    Step 4: Add the ground beef, cooked onions, ketchup or chili sauce or both, italian seasoning, parsley, and salt and pepper to a bowl. Mix until just combined.

    Step 5: Form a 8"x4 loaf on the prepared baking pan and bake for 40 minutes.

    Step 6: While the meatloaf is cooking combine the chili sauce and ketchup (and brown sugar if using). Spread mixture over meatloaf and bake for another 10-15 minutes or until cooked through and the meatloaf reaches an internal temperature of 160F Degrees. Broil for 1-2 minutes if desired.

    Step 7: Let the meatloaf rest for 10 minutes before slicing and serving.

    • Braised Beef Short Ribs

    • Prep Time: 20 minutes
    • Cook Time: 3 hours 30 minutes
    • Total Time: 3 hours 50 minutes
    • Serves: 6
    Ingredients
  • 5 pounds short ribs
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1 teaspoon black pepper
  • 3 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 2 ribs celery diced
  • 1 sweet onion
  • 2 shallots minced
  • 12 baby carrots or 3 medium carrots diced
  • 3 tablespoons all-purpose flour
  • 2 teaspoons salt
  • 1 tablespoon tomato paste
  • 2 tablespoons minced garlic
  • 1 teaspoon dry mustard powder
  • 1/4 teaspoon red pepper flakes
  • 3 cups of red wine of your choice
  • 4 cups beef stock
  • 3 teaspoons thyme
  • 1 1/2 teaspoons of oregano
  • 4 teaspoons of rosemary
  • 2 bay leaves fresh or dry
    		•

    Step 1: Season the short ribs with salt and pepper, covering all sides of the meat.

    Step 2: Heat the oil in a large Dutch oven over medium heat and sear the ribs in batches, 2 or 3 ribs at a time, searing each side for about 45 seconds. Transfer to a plate and finish searing the rest of the meat.

    Step 3: Remove some of the excess oil/fat from the Dutch oven, making sure to leave some. Add the celery, onions, shallots, and carrots, and carrots and caute until soft and translucent, about 5 minutes.

    Step 4: Add in the flour, salt, tomato paste, garlic, mustard powder, and red pepper flakes and stir frequently for about 1 minute.

    Step 5: Add in the wine and use your spoon to scrape and deglaze the bottom of the pot. Add the ribs back in and spoon the wine over them, then allow them to simmer and the wine to reduce for about 20 minutes. Meanwhile, preheat oven to 350F Degrees.

    Step 6: Add in the beef stock and herbs then cover and bake in the oven for about 2 hours and 45 minutes. The meat should be falling off the bone when done.

    Step 7: Once done cooking, remove the ribs. Skim the excess fat off of the top of the sauce, remove bay leaves and discard fat and bay leaves. Enjoy!

    Source: Braised Beef Ribs Recipe

    • Homemade Cinnamon Roll Recipe

    • Prep Time: 20 minutes
    • Cook Time: 1 hour
    • Rise Time: 1 hour 30 minutes
    • Total Time: 2 hours 50 minutes
    • Serves: 15
    Equipment
  • Saucepan
  • Instant Read Thermometer
  • Stan Mixer with dough hook (optional)
    		•
    Ingredients
  • 1/4 cup warm water
  • 1 package active dry yeast or 2 1/4 teaspoons
  • 3/4 cup whole milk
  • 1/3 cup unsalted butter
  • 1/3 cup granulated sugar plus 1 teaspoon
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 3 3/4 to 4 1/4 cups all-purpose flour divided
  • 2 eggs room temperature
    		•
    Filling
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter softened
  • 1 cup brown sugar packed
  • 2 tablespoons cinnamon
    		•
    Frosting
  • 1 1/2 cups powdered sugar or as needed
  • 4 ounces cream cheese softened
  • 1/4 cup unsalted butter softened
  • 1/2 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1/8 teaspoon salt
    		•

    Step 1: Grease a 9x13 pan or baking dish and set aside.

    Step 2: Combine warm water, yeast and 1 teaspook of white sugar in a small bowl. Let the mixture rest for 7 to 10 minutes or until it becomes foamy.

    Step 3: In a medium saucepan, combine the milk, butter, remaining white sugar, and salt. Heat the mixture to 120 to 130F Degrees with a thermometer.

    Step 4: Attach dough hook to a stand mixer and add 2 cups of flour. Add the eggs, warm milk mixture, and the yeast mixture. Mix on medium-low speed until combined.

    Step 5: Turn the micer to medium spead and add the flour 1/2 a cup at a time until the dough forms a soft ball that pulls away from the sides of the bowl. You may not need all of the flour.

    Step 6: Remove the dough from the bowl and knead it on a lightly floured surface until it is smooth and elastic "about 8 minutes).

    Step 7: Brease a large bowl and add the dough. Lightly cover it with a towel and let it rest in a warm place for 1 hour or until it has doubled in size.
    Step 8: Turn the dough onto a lightly floured surface, and with a rolling pin, roll it out into a 15 x 12-inch rectangle.

    Step 9: Spread butter on the dough and sprinkle the brown sugar and cinnamon on top. Roll the dough tightly, starting on the long side. Using a sharp knife or dental floss, cut the log into 15 even pieces and place them in the prepared pan.

    Step 10: Cover rolls with a kitchen towel and let them rise for 30 to 45 minutes. Preheat oven to 375F Degrees.

    Step 11: Brush rolls with additional milk if desired and make for 20-25 minutes.

    Step 12: To prepare the frosting, while the rolls are baking, coming frosting ingredients with a mixer until fluffy.

    Step 13: Allow rolls to cool for about 10-15 minutes and spread frosting on warm rolls.

    Source: Homemade Cinnamon Roll Recipe
